Course Description

ENGL C1000: Academic Reading and Writing

An introductory course in rhetoric that emphasizes clear, effective written communication and preparation of the research paper. Designed to help students improve reading and writing skills for college-level work through engaged, active approaches to texts.

ENGL 28: Intensive College Writing Skills

Emphasizes clear, effective written communication and preparation of the research paper to prepare students for success in college-level composition and reading. Consists of instruction in composition, comprehension and analysis of college-level readings, and basic research skills.

Student Learning Outcomes

  • Read, comprehend, and analyze college-level writing
  • Respond with thesis-driven analytical essays
  • Write research papers using carefully evaluated sources
  • Demonstrate critical thinking through peer review
  • Develop pre-writing strategies and revision skills

Course Structure

Hybrid Format

Each week follows the same basic pattern of activities: We meet in class once per week. The remainder of our course will be conducted online via Canvas and may involve:

  • Watching videos and completing reading assignments
  • Annotating texts and completing short writing assignments
  • Taking quizzes and participating in discussions
  • Attending one-on-one conferences

Requirements

  • All course texts will be Open Educational Resources posted on Canvas
  • Reliable computer and internet access that meets technical requirements
  • A notebook for class notes, in-class writing, brainstorming and essay planning
  • Writing utensils for in-class activities

Evaluation Methods

30%

Participation

Includes reading annotations, discussion boards, in-class discussions, attendance, and in-class writing assignments.

60%

Essays

Includes identity essay, analytical argument essay, research proposal, and research paper with prewriting, rough draft, and peer review.

Detailed Grade Distribution

  • Midterm: 5%
  • Final: 5%
  • Annotations: 7.5%
  • Discussions: 7.5%
  • Attendance: 7.5%
  • In-Class Writing: 7.5%
  • Research Proposal: 6%
  • Identity Essay: 15%
  • Analytical Argument: 18%

Final Grade Scale

A: 90-100% B: 80-89% C: 70-79% D: 60-69% F: Below 60%

Note: Grades will be rounded to the nearest whole number at semester's end.

Course Policies

Attendance

No penalty for first two absences. With your third absence, you will be dropped from the course. Three tardies or early departures equals one absence.

Late Work

Assignments are due at 11:59PM on the indicated date. Work submitted more than one week late will receive a 10% deduction. Dropboxes won't open more than one week in advance.

Academic Integrity

Plagiarism in any form will not be tolerated. Any act of accidental plagiarism will result in an "F" for the assignment until corrected. Deliberate plagiarism will result in a zero.

Inclusivity

Our class respects all ethnicities, genders, religions, sexual orientations, disabilities, and backgrounds. Disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated.
