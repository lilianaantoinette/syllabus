Course Description

ENGL 100B - Composition II

Expository writing course designed for first-year students. Satisfies one of the writing requirements for general education at CSULB. Emphasizes academic discourse, focusing on analytical reading, thesis-driven writing, and rhetorical strategies. Letter grade only (A-F).

Not open for students with credit in: ENGL 100B, ASAM 100B, AFRS 100B, CHLS 104B, AIS 104B. Traditional mode of instruction.

Student Learning Outcomes

  • SLO 1: Compose work using multiple modalities appropriate for particular rhetorical situations. (GELO 3)
  • SLO 2: Assess rhetorical purpose by demonstrating awareness of audience expectations, representing opposing viewpoints accurately and respectfully, and contextualizing claims. (GELOs 5, 6, 7)
  • SLO 3: Develop an academic essay in response to a writing task, indicating a clear topic, focus, and purpose supported by evidence, relevant details, and effectively integrated source materials; apply correctly and understand the assumptions informing citation systems. (GELOs 1, 2, 3, 4)
  • SLO 4: Recognize and make effective use of organizational patterns and transitional devices that serve both the writer's rhetorical purposes and the needs of the reading audience. (GELOs 2, 4)
  • SLO 5: Demonstrate critical comprehension of college-level reading by grasping the significance of the topic, recognizing assumptions, and providing well-developed, informed analysis. (GELOs 6, 7)
  • SLO 6: Demonstrate control of sentence structure, grammar, punctuation, spelling, and mechanics to enhance clarity and credibility; examine writing conventions as rhetorical decisions: negotiable and fluid across various genres and contexts. (GELO 4)

Course Structure

  • Ball, Cheryl. and Drew Loewe. Bad Ideas About Writing. West Virginia University Libraries, 2017. https://textbooks.lib.wvu.edu/badideas/badideasaboutwriting-book.pdf
  • A composition notebook or similar for journal entries

Additional course readings are posted on Canvas as links or PDF files. If you have any trouble finding or opening these documents, please let me know so that I can help you get access.

Requirements

  • All assignments must follow current MLA formatting
  • Writing assignments must be uploaded as Word files (.docx) or Google Docs with version history enabled
  • Submissions will be processed through Turnitin
  • Reliable computer and internet access
  • A composition notebook for handwritten journal entries

Assignments Overview

Rhetorical Response Journal (20%)

Series of ten low-stakes, handwritten journal entries designed to develop critical reading and writing skills through consistent practice and reflection. Graded on engagement and completion rather than correctness.

Position Paper (20%)

750-1000 word essay articulating and defending your vision of the university. Requires applying critical reading, rhetorical awareness, evidence integration, and revision skills. Drafting occurs in stages with version history enabled.

Conference Presentation (10%)

5-7 minute lecture based on your conference paper topic, using at least one visual aid. Followed by a brief reflection analyzing your presentation experience.

Conference Paper (25%)

1500-word capstone project synthesizing the skills developed throughout the semester. Developed from your conference presentation into a polished, persuasive academic paper with a complex thesis.

Quizzes (10%)

Weekly open-note (but not open-computer) quizzes to encourage consistent engagement with course material and check understanding. May be retaken to improve scores.

Participation and Attendance (15%)

Based on consistent presence and active engagement in this workshop-based course. Final grade determined by averaging self-evaluation with peer evaluations.

Course Policies

Attendance

More than three unexcused absences will lower your final grade. Excused absences require documentation for illness, family emergencies, religious reasons, jury duty, or university activities.

Your three free absences are for circumstances like car trouble, minor illness, work conflicts, etc. I recommend saving them for later in the semester.

Late Work

Late submissions incur penalties:

  • Less than 1 hour late: 5% deduction
  • Less than 24 hours late: 10% deduction
  • Less than one week late: 20% deduction
  • More than one week late: maximum grade of 50%
If having technical difficulties, email your work to show it was completed on time, then submit to Canvas when able.

"Life Happens" Extension

Once per semester, each student may use one 48-hour extension without explanation. Just email me to let me know you're using it. Cannot be used for the final conference paper.

Academic Integrity

Plagiarism, self-plagiarism, or unauthorized AI use will result in a zero and possible course failure. Using GenAI tools to generate content is not allowed. All work is screened through Turnitin, and version history is required for non-handwritten assignments.

Communication Policy

I respond to emails within 24 hours Monday-Friday (9-5). Weekend responses aren't guaranteed. Enable Canvas notifications for announcements. Office hours: MW 10-11am in MHB510.

Classroom Conduct

Respectful discussion is expected. Avoid inflammatory language regarding gender, orientation, age, race, culture, or religion. Silence phones and avoid non-course related browsing. Disruptive behavior may result in being asked to leave, counting as an absence.
